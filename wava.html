Details & Analysis
  • Story & Narrative Design:
    The main purpose of WAVA is to transmit a message of growth and self-discovery using mainly environmental storytelling. For instance, if the character is feeling unstable, alone and lost, the weather will be gloomy, bridges will be broken and caves will be more present. The main game loop itself also exemplifies the game's main themes of self-growth and finding your place, since it is based on defeating and overcoming Setbacks, acquiring Knowledge from them and then using it to go further and higher through the world.

  • Gameplay & Level Design:
    Although the game is intended to be easy to play, it still feels engaging by combining puzzles, "combat" and different types of character movement; such as walking, running, crouching, or swimming. On the other hand, the absence of jumping is intended to make the game more grounded. The game dynamic is constantly changing in order to keep the game refreshing and mirror the main character's growth. For instance, when the main character can't protect itself, the game is more focused on logic and running away from Setbacks. The level composition also plays its part, since it will tend upwards or downwards depending on the current mental state of the character. Finally, a collectible system evokes a sense of exploration and gives the game more replayability.

  • UI & UX Design:
    In order to stay in line with the game's cinematic nature, the interface is intended to be as diegetic and non-intrusive as possible. The HUD is updated whenever the player switches their controller, making it possible to alternate easily between keyboard, PlayStation and XBOX controller. Controls are simple, intuitive and follow a similar pattern to other platformers. As of now, control configuration is not possible.

  • Art & Animation:
    The artistic and visual aspect of the game is very minimalist, atmospheric and combines abstractness with realism. Despite its simplicity, thanks to its distinct simple vector art-style, flat colours and approximately 35 different spritesheet animations, the game has a very unique appearance.

  • Sound & Music:
    The original soundtrack was composed by a good friend of mine who is a professional musician. We worked together to craft a sound that complements the game's feel and story. I also focused on elevating the ambience and immersion through the sound design; for example, with reverb inside caves or a lowpass filter when going underwater. The auditory aspect is the only one that was not entirely made by myself.
