Detailed Analysis
  • Narrative Design: The main purpose of WAVA is to transmit a message of self-discovery using only visual storytelling. The story is told mainly through the game loop itself and the game mechanics, as well as with the relationship and dynamic between the two main characters, each one of them being the carrier of a different narrative theme. Aspects like the weather also have deeper narrative importance, along with other level elements such as bridges and caves.

  • Level & Systems Design: Even though the game is intended to be linear and easy to play, it still feels balanced and refreshing as it combines elements of combat, logic, puzzle and different types of movement. The change of the game's dynamic from chapter to chapter mirrors the story and the character's feelings and, in addition, a collectible system evokes a sense of exploration to the player that gives the game more replayability.

  • Art & Animation: As the game itself, the art and visual aspect of the game is minimalist, conceptual and atmospheric. Nonetheless, thanks to its distinct vector art-style and approximately 35 different spritesheet animations, the game looks unique.

Inspiration
  • Game Feel: Limbo & Inside (Playdead, 2010/2016), Gris (Nomada Studio, 2018), Another World (Éric Chahi, 1991)
  • Game Art: Familia (Sebastian Curi, 2016)
  • Game Sound: Gymnopédie No. 1 (Erik Satie, 1888)
