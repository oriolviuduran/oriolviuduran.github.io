Details & Analysis
  • Story & Narrative Design:
    The main purpose of WAVA is to transmit a message of self-discovery using only visual storytelling. The story is told mainly through the game loop and the main mechanics, as well as the relationship and dynamic between the two main characters, each one being the carrier of a different narrative theme. Visual aspects like the weather help enhance the story, along with other level elements such as bridges or caves.

  • Level & Systems Design:
    Although the game is intended to be linear and easy to play, it still feels engaging by combining elements of combat and logic with a somewhat realistic character movement. The game dynamic changes from chapter to chapter so as to keep the game refreshing and to mirror the story. Finally, a collectible system evokes a sense of exploration to more experienced players and gives the game more replayability.

  • UI & UX Design:
    In order to stay in line with the game's cinematic nature, the interface is intended to be as diegetic and non-intrusive as possible. It is also updated whenever the player switches their controller, making it possible to alternate between Keyboard, PlayStation and XBOX controller. Controls are simple, intuitive and follow a similar pattern to other platformers. As of now, control configuration is not possible.

  • Art & Animation:
    The artistic and visual aspect of the game is very minimalist, atmospheric and combines abstractness with realism. Despite its simplicity, thanks to its distinct simple vector art-style, flat colours and approximately 35 different spritesheet animations, the game has a very unique appearance.

  • Sound & Music:
    For the auditory part of the game I worked with a good friend of mine who is a professional musician. We worked together along several in-person sessions to create a sound that complements the game's feel. It is the only aspect that has not been made exclusively by myself.
